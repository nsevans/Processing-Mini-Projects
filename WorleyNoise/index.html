Worley noise is a noise algorithm initialy introduced by Steven Worley. It is used to create procedural textures and it comes close to simulating textures like water, stone and biological cells. The algorithm chooses random points on the canvas, and then it colors each pixel on the screen depending on how close it is to one of the randomly placed points

There are different options to play with at the bottom of the screen that will result in different patterns. Whenever the options change, press the 'Restart Simulation' button to see what effects it will have.
